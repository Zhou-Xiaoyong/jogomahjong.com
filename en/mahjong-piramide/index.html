Pyramid strategy

Mahjong Pyramid rewards the player who sees the structure in 3D. As each upper layer partially covers the layer below, many tiles remain blocked until you remove those above. The difference from traditional solitaire (which uses a turtle layout) is that the pyramid here is more symmetric and compact.

Practical tips

  • Start from the edges: side tiles of the base are usually free from the start.
  • Watch the top: freeing the z=3 layer tiles early opens many options in lower layers.
  • Don't rush to match everything: sometimes it's worth saving a pair to unlock another tile later.
  • Use hint wisely: it doesn't cost time, but if you use it too much you lose the fun.
  • Shuffle is a safety net: if no moves remain, shuffle redistributes remaining tiles without restarting.

Connection with Chinese culture

The stepped pyramid shape resembles ancient Chinese ziggurats, like the Ming dynasty temples in Beijing. In Mahjong, stacking is a visual metaphor for patience and strategy — core values of the traditional game played in Chinese homes and clubs since the Qing dynasty.

Who this mode is for

It's ideal for players who have already beaten classic solitaire and want a different spatial challenge. The pyramid is shorter than the turtle layout (30 pairs vs ~72), so it fits in a quick 5-10 minute break.
